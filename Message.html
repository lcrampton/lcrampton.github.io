On Styrofoam Containers

Hello [establishment]:

I love your food. The [menu item] is really delicious.

However, your use of polystyrene takeout containers is disconcerting to those of us who feel concern for the current state and future of the environment.

Polystyrene is terrible for the environment. It takes hundreds of years to decompose, so it builds up in landfills and waterways. Animals can ingest it, causing damage to their digestive tracts. Health experts suspect that microwaving food in polystyrene containers is unsafe (it melts at around 100 degrees Celsius).

For these reasons, I have reservations about ordering from your establishment, and I will have to withhold my business until this changes. When it does, I intend to be the first back on [menu item] street.

Here are some (inexpensive) alternatives to polystyrene containers:

  • Clamshell containers from Green Paper Products
  • World Centric plant fiber clamshells (and a nifty folding version)
  • PrimeWare sugarcane containers

While you are at it, you may want to get ahead of the curve and stock up on plant-based cutlery as well. The future is, after all, green.

[your name]
