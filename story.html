The Story So Far

Howdy everybody.  My name is Lucas.  I recently stopped "going home" and started "visiting my parents." Now i live in the District of Columbia.  This is where people ostensibly make policy.  This is not New York.

I came here from Allentown, Pennsylvania, first to study, then to find work.  Allentown is a third-tier city.  And as a (commercial) pilot's son who moved a few times, i used to find a little difficulty calling it "home" with a great deal of confidence.  But after twelve years nestled in the Appalachians, it earned the appellation.  After talking down on it for years, i will now defend Allentown earnestly from vebal attack in DC's mean streets.  I will shoot down pejoratives with statistical flak, historical gunfire, and anecdotal torpedoes.  Allentown is my family.  Nobody talks to her like that (except me).

I studied Global Health and Sustainability at the George Washington University.  When i graduated, i lived with my parents for a few months and made The Bibimbap Bop with Big Bill.  When the energy grew too restless, i packed a couple bags and took a bus back to Washington.  There, i began cooking at a restaurant owned by a celebrity chef.  After six months, i left to protest the poor and unethical management.  The restaurant folded ten days later.  I switched to a catering start-up.  The work there indulged my work ethic and tested my physical and mental endurance.  One of my favorite memories from those days involves loading seventy pound CaterGators up a steep hill into a van in a steady downpour while a rivulet flowed through the holes in my shoes.  Some people do this every week for decades.  I lasted three and a half months.

By July of 2018, i felt the need to make moves into the office world of Washington, to continue chasing the dream.  College was my springboard to enhance my power to make the world a better place.  The time i spent in kitchens and schlepping food was time i could do little to ameliorate the condition of the world's most vulnerable or to slow anthropogenic climate change.  So i leapt.  Without a job lined up, i quit my catering job without any hard feelings.  I dusted off the suit and passed out rèsumès.  After a few weeks, i became...an office temporary!  At least i was on the right ladder, though, even if i remained at the bottom rung.

My temporary workings bounced me around a few mailing assistant positions downtown before i landed at a long-term (three months) assignment at the National Kidney Foundation serving the National Capital Area.  I worked on organizing their walk and gala events.  Also, they liked my writing enough to let me compose segments for their monthly newsletter.  As my termination date would grow nearer, they would extend my assignment.  This continued as they encouraged me to apply to the full-time Community Outreach Coordinator position.

And that is what i do now!  Nearly two years after graduation, i have landed a real job that requires a degree.  This walk took a long time—longer than i imagined while sitting in the library or the residence hall.  Whoever you are, wherever you are going, keep your eye on the future and never stop pushing.  Reflect on your strategy and work cleverly.  You are not above anything.  Have the patience to let your work speak for itself.  There are ten thousand lessons in peeling shrimp.

Pittsburgh

I visited Pittsburgh in October 2018.  Pittsburgh is a neat city with blue collar vibes and educated energy.  At least several people of whom you have heard have origins in Pittsburgh: Rachel Carson, Andy Warhol, Mister Rogers, Mac Miller.  Consider it the Grand Rapids of Pennsylvania.  While there, i had the opportunity to go for a couple runs.  Always run on vacation; these parts are some of the most memorable—not for being the best parts, but for the brain chemistry.  And what a way to explore a place.  Anyway, my man VG Money took me around town and showed me the Andy Warhol Museum, the botanical gardens, and some hip spots for pierogies and Izakaya.

Tenallytown

Tenleytown was home for nearly two years.  I watched seasons turn around the trees and schools and churches there.  The setting sun painted Fort Reno gold, scarlet, and orange.  The radio towers always blinked but never flinched.

I hope never to forget the characters of Tenley:

  • Poodle Man
  • Matthew Lesko
  • Black Man with Pit Bull
  • Nanny Wit Da Fanny

A part of me hopes they will remember me.  Or at least think on occasion, "Whatever happened to that handsome runner?" We are irreversibly tied to the geographic areas we and our forefathers inhabit, and i expect to make returns, both by obligation and accident.

I lived in Petworth for a spell. Now i live in Glover Park, which works. 

Africa

Let me take us back to Africa for a little while.  In 2016, i took a sixteen-hour plane ride to Gaborone, Botswana for a semester abroad.  This is something i had really wanted to do, but it is also something we just do.  Doubt began swirling even before my plane ride and compounded after i landed.  Why was i here? What was i to do with no language to learn and a bleak semi-arid zone stretched out before me? I planned a trip east toward Mozambique, hoping that would be the cure, but ten to sixteen men jumped us in Johannesburg.  I ended up in a pretty dark place—an angry, despondent, anxious place—for a lot of that trip.  Even so, there are few elations like those of ziplining across gorges in Swaziland's Malolotja National Park, or spilling from an eight-hour cramped bus ride onto the pristine white sand of Tofo Beach.  Life can be a wild ride sometimes.

Things started clearing up towards the end of my stay, perhaps partly because it was coming to a close.  But i also made a ten-day excursion into Namibia, and that was exciting.  Namibia utterly changed me.  The Earth is a magnificent place.  I asked myself if the sky looks bigger in Africa; it is difficult to explain because it is difficult to comprehend.  Standing in the Sossusvlei, or sitting six feet from flamingos in Walvis Bay, or watching a thunderstorm tumble over the mountains around Windhoek and overtake you, you cannot shake the sense that this planet will one day rise up around you and swallow whole everything humanity holds dear.  The sun beats down.  The frigid ocean rolls in and out.  The wind blows orange sand across your arms and legs, scrubbing away the old you.
