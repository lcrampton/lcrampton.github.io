The Story So Far

Howdy everybody. My name is Lucas. I recently stopped "going home" and started "visiting my parents." Now i live in the District of Columbia, a second-rate city. This is where nobody makes anything and everybody gets paid. This is not New York.

I came here from Allentown, Pennsylvania, first to study, then to find work. Allentown is a third-tier city. And as a (commercial) pilot's son, i used to find a little difficulty calling it "home" with a great deal of confidence. But after twelve years nestled in the Appalachians, it earned the appellation. After talking down on it for years, i will now defend Allentown earnestly from vebal attack in DC's mean streets. I will shoot down pejoratives with statistical flak, historical gunfire, and anecdotal torpedoes. Allentown is my family. Nobody talks to her like that (except me).

I studied Global Health and Sustainability at the George Washington University. When i graduated, i lived with my parents for a few months and made The Bibimbap Bop with Big Bill. When the energy grew too restless, I packed a couple bags and took a bus back to Washington. There, i began cooking at a restaurant owned by a celebrity chef. I left to protest the poor and unethical management and switched to a catering start-up. The restaurant folded ten days later.

By July of 2018, i felt the need to make moves into the office world of Washington, to continue chasing the dream. College was my springboard to enhance my power to make the world a better place. The time i spent in kitchens and schlepping food was time i could do little to ameliorate the condition of the world's most vulnerable or to slow anthropogenic climate change. So now i am...an office temporary! At least i am on the right ladder, though, even if i remain at the bottom rung.
